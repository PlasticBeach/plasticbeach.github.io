What Experts Are Saying

Scientists Around the World Are Decrying Unsustainable Plastic Pollution

  • We need to think about what happens to something after we throw it away! Keep in mind that each of us affects everyone else on Earth, because we all share the land, the ocean and especially the air on what is proving to be a pretty small planet. Let's take care of it.

    Bill Nye

    Science Educator

  • If you were diving, it looked like you had just jumped out of a plane, but our nets were coming up completely choked with plastic pieces - On the Great Pacific Garbage Patch

    Jo Ruxton

    Filmmaker

  • 8 million metric tons of plastic now enter the ocean during a typical year - that's the equivalent of finding five grocery bags full of plastic on every foot of coastline in the 192 countries we examined

    Jenna Jambeck

    University of Georgia Ocean Researcher

  • Plastic production has quadrupled since the 1980s, and wind, waves, and sun break all that plastic into tiny bits. We don't know what this plastic is doing. The plastic is somewhere—in the ocean life, in the depths, or broken down into fine particles undetectable by nets.

    Cozar Cabañas

    Professor of Biology, Cadiz

  • There is a breadth of impacts, including both direct mortality and indirect mortality due to reduced fitness and an animal’s reduced ability to feed or defend itself - a mechanism that prevents ocean plastics from ever reaching these habitats in the first place is absolutely necessary.

    Nicholas Mallos

    Director of the Ocean Conservancy’s Trash-Free Seas
